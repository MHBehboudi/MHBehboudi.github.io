About Me

I am a Ph.D. researcher in Cognition and Neuroscience with a strong foundation in **Electrical Engineering**. My work focuses on leveraging signal processing, statistical analysis, and engineering expertise to advance digital health products through innovative research and development. I specialize in applying computational methods and data analytics to extract meaningful insights from biosignals, with the goal of improving health outcomes. I have a proven track record of leading and contributing to research projects in this domain.

My expertise spans **MATLAB, Python, and R**, and I am proficient in developing, implementing, and optimizing signal processing and machine learning pipelines for large-scale neurophysiological datasets. My background includes a focus on EEG signal processing, connectivity analysis, and developing innovative tools for real-time neural data collection and analysis.

Featured Projects

Mobile EEG Stimuli & Signal System

Led the development of a mobile system for real-time stimulus delivery and EEG data acquisition. This system significantly enhanced the investigation of auditory and cognitive processes in children aged 5 to 15, across various socioeconomic backgrounds.

Learn More →

Connectivity & Graph Theory Analysis

Created pipelines for connectivity and graph theory analysis to study EEG-based neural interactions in language processing tasks. I developed custom MATLAB scripts to compute the Phase Lag Index (PLI) to capture phase synchronization between EEG channels.

Learn More →

Computer-Aided Sleep Apnea Diagnosis System

Developed a diagnosis system for detecting sleep apnea using EEG signals, focusing on complexity features and entropy measures. I utilized the mRMR algorithm for feature selection and SVM and KNN classifiers to accurately identify apnea events.
