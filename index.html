About Us

Welcome to NeuroDynamics Lab. We are at the forefront of **neuroscience research**, specializing in the intricate fields of **biological signal processing** and **advanced statistical analysis**. Our mission is to unravel the complexities of brain function using cutting-edge methodologies, including **EEG, MEG, and fMRI data analysis**. Based in **Dallas, Texas**, our team develops novel analytical tools and applies machine learning techniques to gain deeper insights into neurological processes, from perception and cognition to neurological disorders.

Our expertise spans **time-frequency analysis, source localization, connectivity analysis, and developing custom algorithms** to extract meaningful patterns from noisy biological signals. We are committed to open science and sharing our tools and findings with the broader scientific community.

Neuroscience & Signal Processing Glossary

EEG (Electroencephalography)

A neurophysiological measurement technique that records the electrical activity of the brain from the scalp.

MEG (Magnetoencephalography)

A neurophysiological technique that measures the magnetic fields produced by electrical currents in the brain.

ICA (Independent Component Analysis)

A computational method for separating a multivariate signal into additive subcomponents, often used for artifact removal in EEG/MEG.

Spectrogram

A visual representation of the spectrum of frequencies of a signal as it varies with time, crucial for time-frequency analysis.

Source Localization

The process of estimating the intracranial neuronal sources of activity recorded by EEG or MEG sensors.

Time-Frequency Analysis

Techniques that analyze how the frequency content of a signal changes over time, revealing oscillatory dynamics.
