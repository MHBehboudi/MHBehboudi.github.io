About
Cognition, Code & Connectivity

I specialize in designing well-controlled human experiments and building computational pipelines that extract meaning from high-dimensional neural time-series. My dissertation uses EEG connectivity and graph theory to map how children's large-scale brain networks develop during language processing—specifically, how the brain handles semantic and syntactic errors.

Beyond my thesis, I've developed a novel cross-frequency time-lagged connectivity algorithm (MDPC), won the LibriBrain neural decoding competition with a hybrid CNN–Transformer–GNN architecture, and built automated pipelines that process 500+ GB of EEG data from 100+ participants. I also leverage LLMs and ASR to analyze children's home language environments at scale.
