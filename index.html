Neuroscience & Signal Processing Glossary

EEG (Electroencephalography)

A neurophysiological measurement technique that records the electrical activity of the brain from the scalp.

MEG (Magnetoencephalography)

A neurophysiological technique that measures the magnetic fields produced by electrical currents in the brain.

ICA (Independent Component Analysis)

A computational method for separating a multivariate signal into additive subcomponents, often used for artifact removal in EEG/MEG.

Spectrogram

A visual representation of the spectrum of frequencies of a signal as it varies with time, crucial for time-frequency analysis.

Source Localization

The process of estimating the intracranial neuronal sources of activity recorded by EEG or MEG sensors.

Time-Frequency Analysis

Techniques that analyze how the frequency content of a signal changes over time, revealing oscillatory dynamics.
